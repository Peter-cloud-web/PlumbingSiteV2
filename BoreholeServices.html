Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our borehole services

How long does it take to drill a borehole?

The timeframe for drilling a borehole typically ranges from 3-7 days, depending on factors such as ground conditions, drilling depth, and weather conditions. Our team provides an estimated timeline during the initial consultation based on your specific situation.

How often should a borehole be maintained?

We recommend professional maintenance at least once a year to ensure optimal performance and longevity of your borehole system. However, high-usage systems may require more frequent checks. Regular maintenance helps identify potential issues early and prevents costly repairs.

What permits are required for borehole drilling?

Typically, you'll need permits from local water authorities and environmental agencies. Requirements vary by region, but our consultation service includes guidance on all necessary permits and can assist with the application process to ensure regulatory compliance.

How deep are most boreholes drilled?

Borehole depths vary significantly based on the local hydrogeology. In our region, residential boreholes typically range from 100-300 meters deep. Commercial projects may require deeper drilling. We determine the optimal depth during the site assessment phase to ensure a reliable water supply.

Is borehole water safe to drink?

Borehole water can be safe for drinking, but it requires proper testing and potentially treatment systems. We conduct comprehensive water quality testing after drilling and can recommend appropriate filtration systems if needed. Regular testing is part of our maintenance service to ensure continued water safety.
