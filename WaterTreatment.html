Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our water treatment services

How often should I test my water quality?

We recommend testing your water annually for private wells and every 2-3 years for municipal water supplies. However, you should test immediately if you notice changes in taste, odor, or appearance, or after any plumbing work or contamination events in your area.

What contaminants can water treatment systems remove?

Our water treatment systems can remove a wide range of contaminants including bacteria, viruses, chlorine, heavy metals, pesticides, sediment, hardness minerals, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs). The specific contaminants removed depend on the type of treatment system installed.

How do I know if I need a water softener?

Signs you may need a water softener include: white mineral deposits on fixtures, soap scum buildup, dry skin and hair after bathing, reduced lather from soaps and detergents, and frequent appliance repairs due to scale buildup. A professional water test will confirm hardness levels.

How long do water treatment systems last?

With proper maintenance, most water treatment systems last 10-15 years. Reverse osmosis systems typically need membrane replacement every 2-3 years, while water softeners can last 15-20 years with regular maintenance. We provide comprehensive maintenance plans to maximize system lifespan.

What's included in a comprehensive water test?

Our comprehensive water tests include analysis for bacteria, pH levels, hardness, chlorine, heavy metals (lead, mercury, copper), nitrates, pesticides, and volatile organic compounds. We also test for physical properties like turbidity and total dissolved solids, providing a complete water quality profile.
